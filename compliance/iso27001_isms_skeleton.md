# ISO 27001:2022 ISMS Skeleton — CortexView

This skeleton ISMS maps the Annex A controls relevant to a self-hosted
CortexView deployment. Use it as a starting point when responding to
ISO 27001-style customer questionnaires.

## Scope

The Information Security Management System covers the operation of
the CortexView platform within {{ORG_NAME}}. Out of scope: cloud
provider physical security (covered by the underlying cloud's own
ISO 27001 attestation).

## Risk register

The platform's current risk register (per Cl. 6.1) is maintained
in-product under **Compliance → Evidence → Risk register** (planned
v1.5+ surface — for now, see `risk_register.md` in this directory).

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compromise of `ENCRYPTION_KEY` | Low | High | OPERATOR.md §7 rotation; KMS envelope option (v2.0) |
| Mass account compromise | Low | High | MFA, lockout, IP allowlist, SSO-only mode |
| Webhook receiver compromise | Medium | Medium | HMAC signing, 24h secret-rotation overlap |
| LLM provider data leak | Low | Medium | Operator chooses provider; no telemetry from CV itself |
| Stale soft-deleted data | Low | Low | Retention sweeper, `record.purged_by_retention` audit |

## Internal audit cadence

- Quarterly: review the audit log for `permission.grant.created`,
  `impersonation.start`, and `org.saml.configure` events.
- Half-yearly: review the risk register; update mitigations.
- Yearly: full ISMS re-certification audit (external auditor).

## Management review

Senior leadership reviews the platform's security posture via the
Compliance Evidence bundle (`compliance/evidence/{period}.tar.gz`).
The bundle is generated by the nightly cron in
`queues/complianceEvidenceQueue.ts`.

## Continual improvement

Findings from {audit / penetration test / vulnerability disclosure} are
filed in the risk register and tracked through the Jira / ServiceNow
notification-channel adapters (v2.0+). Closure requires audit-log
evidence (`record.updated`, `policy.update`, etc.).
